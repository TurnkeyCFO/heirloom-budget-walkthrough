01 · The picture in one breath

2026 is strong. 2027 is the test. The plan is buildable — but it depends on three real choices.

The model carries actual numbers from 2025 and early 2026, then forecasts every assumption — attendance, giving, hires, rent, partner funding — through June 2028. Numbers below are the current plan, not a hope.

The good news

Internal tithes climb from $198k in 2025 to $416k in 2027, driven by attendance growth (85 → 210 avg) and the second-service launch. 2026 holds at a ~$74k surplus; 2027 lands +$27k instead of the −$10k base plan.

The investment

Two services launch Jan 2027 (extra opex ~$1k/mo for setup, worship, family ministry). Dedicated location at $4,500/mo from Sep 2027 — that's $1k/mo more than the prior model assumed, but it unlocks permanence and lifts giving culture.

The decision

The scenario flips 2027 from −$10k to +$27k and 2028 H1 from −$23k to +$10k. The catch: every assumption depends on the second-service plant actually filling. The five levers below stay relevant — they just move faster from this baseline.

06 · Why operating cash tightens

Four real forces are pressing on cash at the same time.

None of these are surprises and none are emergencies. They're the natural consequence of growing: bigger team, bigger venue, less outside help. Knowing them lets us plan against them.

1

Partner cliff

External partner gifts: $155k → $142k → $19k → $0 across 2025–2028 H1. By design — partners aren't permanent — but the gap has to be replaced.

2

Personnel ramp

Total comp moves from ~$176k to $232k by 2027 as Noble shifts to full-time (Jul 2027), retirement contributions begin, and a 3% COL kicks in.

3

Dedicated location

Sunday rent: $1,500/mo → $4,500/mo starting Sep 2027 when Heirloom moves to a dedicated space. About $36k/yr additional once fully on — but unlocks 2nd-service capacity and giving culture.

4

Two-service launch costs

From Jan 2027 the second service adds roughly $1,000/mo in worship, family ministry, and setup labor. The bet is attendance and per-cap giving rise enough to more than cover it.

07 · What reverses it

Five levers, each with a real dollar value attached.

These are the buttons we can press. They aren't all-or-nothing — pulling two of the smaller ones often gets us where one big one would.

A

New partners to fill the gap

Each new partner at $25k/yr closes a year's deficit cleanly. Two new partners = comfortable surplus through 2027.

Owner: Pastoral relationships

B

Per-attendee giving past $172

Scenario assumes per-cap rises from $160 → $165 (2027) → $172 (2028) as the dedicated space matures giving culture. Every extra $5/att/mo at 210 attendees = $12.6k/yr.

Owner: Generosity teaching + onboarding

C

Second-service fill rate

Scenario expects 2027 avg attendance of 210 (from 185 base) — a +14% lift. Every 10 attendees short of that target = ~$19k/yr revenue gap.

Owner: Outreach, ministry quality, launch team

D

Negotiate the dedicated space below $4,500

Each $500/mo off the dedicated rent = $6k/yr. Pushing the Sep-2027 move to early 2028 saves ~$16k (4 months × $4k delta) — but delays the giving lift.

Owner: Pastor / venue search

E

Defer Noble's full-time move

If Noble stays part-time through end of 2027 (vs. Jul 2027), saves $10–12k. Real trade-off: worship leadership capacity right when the second service launches.

Owner: Eldership decision

Reserves are the safety net — not the runway.

$180k sits in reserves earning ~2% (~$3,600/yr). Surplus from 2026 lifts it; the scenario surplus from 2027 + 2028 H1 (~$48k combined) flows in too once the 2.5×-trailing-expense operating cushion is hit. By June 2028, reserves are projected at ~$278k. The discipline of the plan is to not pull from reserves to cover operating shortfalls — they stay intact for a real emergency or a strategic, board-approved investment.

08 · How to read this in one sentence

Heirloom is moving from "partner-supported" to "congregation-supported." 2026 proves the model. The 2-service launch + dedicated space turn 2027 from a stretch into a growth year.

The numbers above aren't predictions — they're a plan. The scenario applied here (2 services Jan 2027, dedicated location Sep 2027 at $4,500/mo) makes both moves cash-positive provided attendance and per-cap giving land roughly where the model expects.

What's already true

2025 closed at $379k revenue, $420k expense. 2026 is on track for $469k revenue and a $74k surplus.

What we're watching

Second-service launch attendance, dedicated-space lease terms, and per-cap giving response — those three signals tell us if the scenario holds.

What changes monthly

Actuals reconcile against budget every week. The plan adjusts each quarter as drivers update.
