01 · The picture in one breath

2026 is strong. 2027 is the test. The plan is buildable — but it depends on three real choices.

The model carries actual numbers from 2025 and early 2026, then forecasts every assumption — attendance, giving, hires, rent, partner funding — through June 2028. Numbers below are the current plan, not a hope.

The good news

Internal tithes are on a steep climb — from $198k in 2025 to a forecast $355k in 2027, driven by attendance growth from 85 → 185 average Sundays. 2026 is on track for a ~$74k surplus.

The crunch

External partner gifts taper hard: $155k → $19k from 2025 to 2027, then zero. Personnel costs ramp (Noble goes full-time mid-2027). Sunday rent steps from $1.5k to $4.5k/mo in Sep 2027.

The decision

Whether 2027 closes flat or in deficit comes down to five levers: new partner funding, per-attendee giving, attendance growth, timing of the rent step, and timing of Noble's full-time move. Each has a number behind it.

06 · Why operating cash tightens

Four real forces are pressing on cash at the same time.

None of these are surprises and none are emergencies. They're the natural consequence of growing: bigger team, bigger venue, less outside help. Knowing them lets us plan against them.

1

Partner cliff

External partner gifts: $155k → $142k → $80k → $30k across 2025–2028 H1. By design — partners aren't permanent — but the gap has to be replaced.

2

Personnel ramp

Total comp moves from ~$176k to $232k by 2027 as Noble shifts to full-time (Jul 2027), retirement contributions begin, and a 3% COL kicks in.

3

Rent step-up

Sunday rent: $1,500/mo → $4,500/mo starting Sep 2027 — new venue / second service. About $36k/yr additional once fully on.

4

Internal giving must catch up

At $160/attendee/mo and the planned attendance curve, internal tithes do grow — but they need to grow faster than partners + rent + payroll combined.

07 · What reverses it

Five levers, each with a real dollar value attached.

These are the buttons we can press. They aren't all-or-nothing — pulling two of the smaller ones often gets us where one big one would.

A

New partners to fill the gap

Each new partner at $25k/yr closes a year's deficit cleanly. Two new partners = comfortable surplus through 2027.

Owner: Pastoral relationships

B

Per-attendee giving up to $185

Moving the per-attendee dial from $160 → $185 (+15.6%) at 155 attendees adds about $46k/yr. Single biggest lever.

Owner: Generosity teaching + onboarding

C

Attendance growth

Every additional 10 attendees at $160/mo = $19,200/yr. The 2028 jump from 155 → 195 is where the model hits cash positive.

Owner: Outreach, ministry quality

D

Defer or phase the rent step

Pushing the Sep-2027 rent jump to mid-2028 saves roughly $30k. Or land a venue closer to $3k/mo and the math changes immediately.

Owner: Pastor / venue search

E

Defer Noble's full-time move

If Noble stays part-time through end of 2027 (vs. Jul 2027), saves $10–12k. Real trade-off: worship leadership capacity for the new venue.

Owner: Eldership decision

Reserves are the safety net — not the runway.

$180k sits in reserves earning ~2% (~$3,600/yr). Surplus from 2026 lifts it; modest interest carries it. By June 2028, reserves are projected at $229k. The discipline of the plan is to not pull from reserves to cover operating shortfalls — they stay intact for a real emergency or a strategic, board-approved investment.

08 · How to read this in one sentence

Heirloom is moving from "partner-supported" to "congregation-supported." 2026 proves the model. 2027 is the year the levers matter most.

The numbers above aren't predictions — they're a plan. As actuals come in each month, we update the model and revisit which levers are still ours to pull. Nothing here changes without us deciding it changes.

What's already true

2025 closed at $379k revenue, $420k expense. 2026 is on track for $469k revenue and a $74k surplus.

What we're watching

Per-attendee giving, partner pipeline, and venue conversations — those three signals decide 2027.

What changes monthly

Actuals reconcile against budget every week. The plan adjusts each quarter as drivers update.
